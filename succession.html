IV–V. Nineteenth‑Century Succession & Collateral Branches (1857–1930)

James Keary (c.1813–1886). Died 19 Sep 1886, farmer, Durhamstown; age 73; Luke Keary, informant (4). This aligns with the Revision transfer from Thomas → James and anchors Luke as an adult witness within the farm circle.

Thomas Keary (c.1838–1909). Died 5 Jun 1909, Liscarton; occupation “herd”; informant Hugh Keary, brother (5). The occupational note matches late‑Victorian estate patterns in Meath.

Thomas Keary (c.1869–1930). Died 24 Dec 1930, Durhamstown; informant Christopher Keary, brother (6). This links directly to the Bohermeen burial group where Christopher and Annie appear.

Hugh & Christopher (collaterals). Hugh Keary (Durhamstown) died 13 Feb 1907, age 83; informant Annie Keary, niece (7). Hugh Keary (Betaghstown) died 25 Jan 1943, age 72; informant Mrs Annie Mullon, niece (8). Christopher Keary died 16 Aug 1945, age 80; informant Joseph O’Byrne, occupier (9). The persistent “Annie” tie binds Durhamstown and Betaghstown households.

Bohermeen plot. St Ultan’s, Plot 294B, records Anne (Annie) Keary (Feb 1943), Christopher (Aug 1945), Thomas (Aug 1954), and Jane (Oct 1969), consolidating the mid‑century cluster (10).
