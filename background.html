II. Historical and Cultural Background (c.1700–1800)

The Keary / Kearey name descends from Ó Ciardha, a southern Uí Néill line in the midlands. Through eighteenth‑century Meath, Catholic tenant strategies were shaped by estate economies (Earl of Essex holdings at Liscarton), tithe exactions, and the slow relief of penal disabilities. By 1800, parish clusters at Martry and Ardbraccan supported literate commemoration and intermarrying neighbour networks.

Economic rhythms—tillage, flax bounties (Martry 1796), and periodic distress—help explain occupational mixes later visible in records: farmers who were also herds, outdoor servants, or day‑labourers; daughters in domestic service; and sons migrating to Dublin, then London (¹).
