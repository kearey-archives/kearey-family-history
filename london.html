VIII. The Dublin & London Offshoot (1791–Present): Migration, Anglicisation, and Five Generations

Dublin 1791: Two baptisms of Thomas Keary—St Paul’s (COI; parents James & Mary) and St Mary’s (RC; parents James Keary & Catherine Byrne); the London line descends from the St Paul’s child as shown by later Anglican rites (³⁰).

London c.1816→: Thomas Kearey marries Hester/Esther Pepler at St Anne, Soho (1819), occupations: baggage porter → gold refiner → goldsmith; surname regularised as Kearey under metropolitan clerks/guilds (³¹).

Five generations: Thomas (bapt. 1791) → Thomas Henry (1820–1867) m. Hannah Raybould → Alfred (1854–1917; carpenter; KIA 1917) m. Martha Sutton → Albert Edward (1889–1971; Royal Engineers 1914–1920) m. Elsie May Collins (1908–1969) → Terence Arthur (b. 1935). WWI losses (Alfred and three sons) contracted the household but the line persisted via Albert Edward → Terence (³²).

Interpretation: The offshoot embodies artisan mobility and orthographic accommodation (Kearey), while preserving Meath naming and Catholic associations.
