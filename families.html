VI–VII. Associated Families, Census Transcriptions, and Local Networks

James Keary & Bridget (“Biddy”) Coyle. Civil births record children of James Keary and Bridget (Biddy) Coyle: Luke (1866; mother “formerly Coyle”) (¹²), Patt (1868) (¹³), Hugh (1870) (¹⁴), and Bridget (1873) (¹⁵). These entries standardise the mother’s maiden surname as Coyle (not Boyle), resolving earlier transcription noise. The cluster aligns with Ongenstown/Durhamstown households in the 1901/1911 censuses.

Luke Keary & Mary Leonard. The 1886/7 marriage of Luke Keary and Mary Leonard is followed by births of William (1888) (¹⁶), Patrick (1893) (¹⁷), and James (1897) (¹⁸). Mary’s origins are consistent with the Leonard households enumerated in Ardbraccan/Martry (1901) (¹⁹), strengthening the local kin ecology.

William Keary & Elizabeth Smith. In 1925, William Keary married Elizabeth Smith; the certificate names father “Luke Keary,” providing a secure inter‑generational link (²⁰). The line remains within the Navan–Ardbraccan orbit through the mid‑century.

Foley & Polian connections. The 1901 Kilmessan census lists the household of Francis Foley (head) and Bridget (wife), with mother Margaret (73) and children including Jane (age 1) (²¹). By 1911, Jane Foley (age 11) appears as “niece” in the Edoxtown household of Peter Polian and Jane (²²). Jane Foley later dies in 1969; informant Kathleen Martin, niece (²³). These data explain the recurring “Foley/Polian” mentions in Keary certificates as kin‑by‑marriage or guardianship bonds.

Betaghstown household (1901/1911). A Keary family group at Betaghstown (James, Julia, and children) appears in both censuses, and dovetails with the 1940s Bohermeen burials and the informant “Annie Mullon” connection (²⁴).
