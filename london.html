VIII. The Dublin & London Offshoot (1791–Present): Migration, Orthography, Mobility

Dublin baptisms (1791). Two registrations for a boy Thomas Keary: St Paul’s (COI), parents James & Mary; St Mary’s Pro‑Cathedral (RC), parents James Keary & Catherine Byrne (sponsors James Dardis & Jane Nowlan). The London branch derives from the St Paul’s child on later Anglican rites and London civil documentation (23).

To London (c.1816). Thomas Kearey marries Hester (Esther) Pepler at St Anne, Soho (1819), working across baggage porter → gold refiner → goldsmith. The surname settles as Kearey, an administrative anglicisation aligned with guild and registry norms.

Lineage. Thomas Kearey (bapt. 1791) → Thomas Henry Kearey (1820–1867) m. Hannah Raybould → Alfred Kearey (1854–1917) m. Martha Sutton → Albert Edward Kearey (1889–1971; Royal Engineers 1914–1920) m. Elsie May Collins (1908–1969) → Terence Arthur Kearey (b. 1935).

War losses. Alfred and three sons (Alfred Ernest, Frederick Augustus, Sidney Reginald) died in WWI, contracting the artisan household and accelerating post‑war relocation patterns.

Interpretation. The offshoot’s social ascent—apprenticeship into precious‑metal crafts—coexisted with service labour (baggage porter). Orthographic regularisation (Kearey) did not efface origin but reframed it for metropolitan institutions.
