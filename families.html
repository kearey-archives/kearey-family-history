VI–VII. Associated Families, Census Transcriptions, and Local Networks

James Keary & Bridget (“Biddy”) Coyle. Birth registrations standardise the mother’s maiden name: Luke (1866), Patt (1868), Hugh (1870), Bridget (1873), each citing “formerly Coyle” (11–14). This household anchors Ongenstown/Durhamstown through the 1870s.

Luke Keary & Mary Leonard. Marriage in 1886/7 (Leonard father Nicholas), followed by births of William (1888), Patrick (1893), James (1897) (15–17). The Leonard households in Ardbraccan/Martry (1901) with John (50), Mary (38), Elizabeth (15), Patrick (11) parallel the Keary footprint geographically.

William Keary & Elizabeth Smith (1925). Marriage certificate names father “Luke Keary,” corroborating the inter‑generational chain (18).

Foley & Polian households. 1901 Kilmessan house 18: Francis Foley (head), Bridget (wife), mother Margaret (73), children William (10), Julia (8), Maggie (6), Mary (4), Jane (1) (19). By 1911 at Edoxtown: Peter Polian (58), Jane (56), with Jane Foley (11) as “niece,” and James Magrane (74), “uncle” (20). Jane Foley appears in Bohermeen plot and dies 1969 (21).

Betaghstown Kearys. 1901/1911 transcripts show James & Julia Keary with children Patrick, Julia, Anne, John, Margret, Thomas, Ellen (and Teresa by 1911), matching later mid‑century informant patterns (22).
