IX. Socioeconomic, Religious, and Cultural Commentary & References

Tenure. The Essex estate tenancy at Liscarton (Griffith’s; Revision succession “Dead → to James”) exemplifies Catholic continuity through reform. Comparable holdings often fragmented; the Keary case shows stability and adaptation.

Religion. Overlapping COI/RC records in Dublin 1791 are pragmatic in context of migration and parish boundaries. Rural Meath records remain largely RC for the farm line.

Labour. Herding, outdoor service (1821 Dormstown), and London metal trades form a coherent skill spectrum driven by estate economy and metropolitan opportunity.

Kinship care. Niece informants (Annie Mullon/Mullins; Jane Foley) and shared Bohermeen burial confirm a strong collateral network into the mid‑twentieth century.
