Overview. Full Expanded Archival Research Edition

This site presents the complete, long-form reconstruction of the Keary / Kearey family of County Meath (c.1730–1969), integrating headstones at Martry and Bohermeen, Griffith’s and Revision Books for Liscarton/Durhamstown, civil registrations (deaths 1886, 1907, 1909, 1930, 1943, 1945; marriages 1886/7, 1925; births 1866–1897), parish baptisms (Dublin 1791), and full household transcriptions from the 1821, 1901, and 1911 censuses. Each generational link is established with explicit citation and a brief analytical note—Proven, Probable, or Possible—following standard genealogical practice.

The narrative is arranged in nine parts: I–III (Origins and early settlement at Martry → Durhamstown/Liscarton), IV–V (nineteenth-century succession and collaterals across Durhamstown, Betaghstown, Bohermeen), VI–VII (associated families—Leonard, Foley, Polian, O’Byrne—plus census tables and cross-reference), VIII (the Dublin & London offshoot beginning with Thomas Kearey, baptised Dublin 1791, who settled in Soho by 1819 and whose descendants continue via Terence Arthur Kearey b.1935), IX (contextual commentary on land, religion, labour, and naming).
