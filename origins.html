I. Introduction

The surname Keary / Kearey derives from Gaelic Ó Ciardha. The earliest secure ancestor is Luke Keary (c.1731–1806) of Martry, commemorated with wife Elizabeth Rooney and daughter Anne (¹). The inscription indicates literacy and a stable Catholic tenant household.

II. Origins & Eighteenth‑Century Context

Within the Essex Winter Estate, mixed farming and parish networks sustained families like the Kearys. By the late eighteenth century, headstones, leaseholds, and local registers situate the family between Martry, Ardbraccan, and Liscarton (¹, ²).

III. Early Durhamstown Settlement (c.1730–1857)

Thomas Keary (c.1766–1857), son of Luke, is recorded in Griffith’s Valuation (c.1854) at Liscarton (house, offices, ~13½ acres). Revision Books mark “Dead → to James Keary,” proving succession on the same plot (²). The 1821 census (Dormstown, House 108) lists Thomas & Anne with children, consistent with a modestly prosperous farm.
