VI–VII. Associated Families, Census Transcriptions, and Local Networks

James Keary & Bridget (“Biddy”) Coyle. Civil births standardise the mother’s maiden surname as Coyle: Luke (1866), Patt (1868), Hugh (1870), Bridget (1873) (11–14). This family centres on Ongenstown/Durhamstown.

Luke Keary & Mary Leonard. Marriage in 1886/7 is followed by births of William (1888), Patrick (1893), James (1897) (15–17). Leonard households in Ardbraccan/Martry (1901) corroborate the locality alignment.

William Keary & Elizabeth Smith (1925). The certificate names father “Luke Keary,” securing the inter‑generational tie (18).

Foley & Polian households. Kilmessan (1901) lists the household of Francis Foley and Bridget, with mother Margaret and children including Jane (age 1) (19). By 1911 Jane Foley (11) appears as “niece” with Peter and Jane Polian at Edoxtown (20). Jane Foley later dies in 1969; informant Kathleen Martin, niece (21).

Betaghstown Kearys (1901/1911). The James/Julia Keary group at Betaghstown with children (Patrick, Julia, Anne, John, Margret, Thomas, Ellen; and later Teresa) fits the Bohermeen plot and the 1940s informant pattern (22).
