IV–V. Nineteenth‑Century Succession & Collateral Branches (1857–1930)

James (†1886) of Durhamstown. Civil register: 19 Sep 1886, farmer, Durhamstown; age 73; informant Luke Keary (4). This supplies both the succession from Thomas and the presence of an adult Luke as kin informant.

Thomas (†1909) of Liscarton. Civil register: 5 Jun 1909, occupation “herd”; informant Hugh Keary, brother (5). The herding label reflects labour diversification under estate agriculture.

Thomas (†1930) of Durhamstown. Civil register: 24 Dec 1930, farmer; informant Christopher Keary, brother (6). The “Christopher” forename threads into 1940s records and Bohermeen burials.

Hugh & Christopher. Hugh (Durhamstown) died 13 Feb 1907, age 83; informant Annie Keary, niece (7). Hugh (Betaghstown) died 25 Jan 1943, age 72; informant Mrs Annie Mullon, niece (8). Christopher died 16 Aug 1945, age 80; informant Joseph O’Byrne, occupier (9). The repeating “Annie” suggests a collateral line binding Durhamstown and Betaghstown.

Bohermeen Plot B‑294. St Ultan’s register lists Anne (Annie) Keary (Feb 1943), Christopher (Aug 1945), Thomas (Aug 1954), Jane (Oct 1969) (10), corroborating a mid‑century family unit.
