I. Introduction

The earliest secure anchor is Luke Keary (c.1731–1806) of Martry; his headstone records Elizabeth Rooney (†1813) and Anne (†1800) (¹).

II. Origins & Eighteenth‑Century Context

Keary households held leaseholds under the Earl of Essex (Winter estate). Literacy and parish culture are reflected in the long Martry inscription (¹).

III. Early Durhamstown Settlement (c.1730–1857)

Thomas Keary (c.1766–1857) is in Griffith’s Valuation (c.1854) at Liscarton; revision books note “Dead → to James Keary” (²). In 1821 (Dormstown House 108) Thomas & Anne appear with children Luke, Thomas (inf.), Anne, Bridget.
