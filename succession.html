IV–V. Nineteenth‑Century Succession & Collateral Branches (1857–1930)

James of Durhamstown. The civil register records James Keary, farmer of Durhamstown, died 19 Sep 1886 (age 73); informant Luke Keary, present (4). This aligns with the Revision entry that the Liscarton plot passed from Thomas to James.

Thomas of Liscarton. Thomas Keary died 5 Jun 1909, Liscarton, occupation “herd”; informant Hugh Keary, brother (5). The occupational note corroborates the herding/labour mix typical of late‑Victorian estate agriculture.

Thomas of Durhamstown. Thomas Keary, farmer, died 24 Dec 1930, Durhamstown; informant Christopher Keary, brother (6). “Christopher” recurs in later documents and in Bohermeen burial data.

Collateral figures. Hugh Keary (Durhamstown) died 13 Feb 1907, age 83; informant Annie Keary, niece (7). Another Hugh (Betaghstown) died 25 Jan 1943, age 72; informant Mrs Annie Mullon, niece (8). Christopher Keary died 16 Aug 1945, age 80; informant Joseph O’Byrne, occupier (9). Repetition of a niece “Annie” across decades (pre‑ and post‑marriage) strongly implies a stable collateral household nexus.

Bohermeen Plot B‑294. St Ultan’s (Bohermeen) lists Anne (Annie) Keary (Feb 1943), Christopher (Aug 1945), Thomas (Aug 1954), and Jane (Oct 1969) in one plot (10), reinforcing the Betaghstown–Bohermeen continuity.
