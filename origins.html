I. Introduction

Earliest secure ancestor: Luke Keary (c.1731–1806) of Martry, with Elizabeth Rooney and Anne, commemorated on a detailed headstone (¹). The inscription’s length implies literacy and stability.

II. Origins & Eighteenth‑Century Context

Within the Essex estate, Catholic tenant families endured restrictions but developed literacy and capital through mixed farming. Parish networks and hedge schools supported record‑keeping (¹,²).

III. Early Durhamstown Settlement (c.1730–1857)

Thomas Keary (c.1766–1857) appears in Griffith’s Valuation (c.1854) at Liscarton; Revision Books state “Dead → to James Keary,” proving direct succession (²). The 1821 Dormstown household (House 108) lists Thomas & Anne with children and servants—consistent with a competent holding.
