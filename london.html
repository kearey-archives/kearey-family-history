VIII. The Dublin & London Offshoot (1791–Present): Anglicisation & Social Mobility

Two Dublin baptisms (1791). St Paul’s (COI)—Thomas Keary, parents James & Mary; St Mary’s (RC)—Thomas, parents James Keary & Catherine Byrne (sponsors James Dardis & Jane Nowlan). The London line derives from the St Paul’s child (23).

London settlement. By 1819 Thomas Kearey marries Hester (Esther) Pepler at St Anne, Soho; careers noted across baggage porter → gold refiner → goldsmith (24). Orthography regularises as Kearey under metropolitan clerks/guild norms.

Descent. Thomas Kearey (bapt. 1791) → Thomas Henry (1820–1867) m. Hannah Raybould → Alfred (1854–1917; carpenter; KIA France 1917) m. Martha Sutton → Albert Edward (1889–1971; Royal Engineers 1914–1920) m. Elsie May Collins (1908–1969) → Terence Arthur (b. 1935).

WWI contraction. Alfred and three sons (Alfred Ernest, Frederick Augustus, Sidney Reginald) died during WWI (25). Surviving line continues via Albert Edward → Terence.

Interpretation. The artisan offshoot rises through skilled craft while maintaining Meath‑derived naming patterns. The added “e” in Kearey reflects pronunciation cues and registry standardisation rather than identity rupture.
